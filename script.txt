**1. Birth and Early Life:**
- **Birth:** Lord Krishna was born in Mathura, India, to Vasudeva and Devaki. His birth is celebrated as Janmashtami. According to the prophecy, he was destined to defeat King Kamsa, who was a tyrant ruler of Mathura.
- **Escape to Gokul:** To protect Krishna from King Kamsa, who sought to kill him, Vasudeva carried the newborn Krishna across the Yamuna River to Gokul. Krishna was then raised by Nanda and Yashoda, who were cowherds.

**2. Childhood and Miracles:**
- **Early Miracles:** Krishna exhibited divine qualities from a young age. He performed miracles such as lifting the Govardhan Hill to protect the villagers from Indra’s anger and defeating various demons like Putana and Trinavarta who were sent by Kamsa to kill him.
- **Leelas in Vrindavan:** Krishna’s childhood is celebrated for his playful acts (Leelas), including stealing butter (Makhan Chori), playing the flute, and performing enchanting dances with the gopis (milkmaids).

**3. Divine Love with Radha:**
- **Radha:** Radha is considered Krishna’s supreme beloved, embodying the divine feminine energy. Their love represents the ultimate union of the soul with the divine. The relationship between Krishna and Radha is symbolic of the eternal and unconditional love between the devotee and God.
- **Rasa Lila:** The Rasa Lila dance, where Krishna danced with the gopis, signifies the spiritual joy and divine love shared between God and his devotees. This dance is a central theme in devotional literature and art.

**4. Adulthood and Role in Mahabharata:**
- **Kingdom of Dwarka:** Krishna later established the city of Dwarka in Gujarat, where he ruled as a king and established the Yadava dynasty. His reign was marked by peace and prosperity.
- **Mahabharata:** Krishna played a crucial role in the epic Mahabharata. He was a charioteer and advisor to Prince Arjuna during the Kurukshetra War. His teachings to Arjuna are encapsulated in the Bhagavad Gita.

**5. Bhagavad Gita:**
- **Teachings:** The Bhagavad Gita is a 700-verse Hindu scripture that is part of the Mahabharata. It is a dialogue between Krishna and Arjuna, addressing the moral and philosophical dilemmas faced by Arjuna. Krishna imparts spiritual wisdom and guidance on duty (Dharma), righteousness, and the paths to spiritual liberation (Moksha).
- **Key Concepts:** The Gita discusses various paths to spiritual realization, including Karma Yoga (the path of action), Bhakti Yoga (the path of devotion), and Jnana Yoga (the path of knowledge).

**6. Later Life and Departure:**
- **Return to the Divine:** Krishna’s life on earth concluded with his departure from his earthly abode, marking the end of the Dvapara Yuga and the beginning of the Kali Yuga, the current age according to Hindu cosmology.
- **Legacy:** Krishna's life and teachings have had a profound impact on Hindu philosophy, devotion, and practice. He is worshipped as the Supreme God in various traditions, and his stories and teachings continue to inspire millions of devotees around the world.

**7. Worship and Festivals:**
- **Bhakti Movement:** Krishna's teachings have greatly influenced the Bhakti movement, which emphasizes devotion and love for God. Various saints and poets, including Mirabai, Chaitanya Mahaprabhu, and others, have contributed to the devotional literature and practices focused on Krishna.
- **Festivals:** Key festivals celebrating Krishna include Janmashtami (his birthday), Holi (the festival of colors), and Radha Ashtami (celebrating Radha). These festivals are marked with devotional singing, dancing, and rituals.
